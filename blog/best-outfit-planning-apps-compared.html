Outfit Planning Apps Compared: Stylebook vs Whering vs indyx vs Pureple vs FitWardrobe

Finding the right digital wardrobe app can take some exploration. With options like Stylebook, Whering, Indyx, and Pureple available, it helps to understand how they differ in functionality and approach.

Is Stylebook worth the one-time fee? Is Whering the right free option for you? And how does FitWardrobe compare?

In this comparison, we break down several popular outfit planning apps of 2026 to help you make an informed choice.

1. Stylebook (The OG Favorite)

The Verdict: A comprehensive tool for data lovers with a focus on granular tracking.

  • Pros: Incredible statistics (cost-per-wear, least worn), massive customization, one-time payment.
  • Cons: iOS only. The interface feels dated (think 2015). Manual background removal can be tedious. No true AI outfit generation (just a random shuffle).
  • Best For: Users who enjoy detailed data and use an iPhone.

2. Whering (The "Clueless" Wardrobe)

The Verdict: Fun, trendy, and social also known as the "Clueless" wardrobe app.

  • Pros: Completely free. Great "Clueless"-style aesthetic. Social features to see friends' closets. Good automatic background removal.
  • Cons: Can be buggy. Heavy focus on shopping sustainable brands (which might tempt you to buy more).
  • Best For: Gen Z users and social butterflies who want a fun, free experience.

3. Indyx (The Professional Stylist)

The Verdict: A digital wardrobe platform that facilitates connections with professional stylists.

  • Pros: Clean, minimalist design. The main selling point is access to human stylists who will digitize your wardrobe (for a fee) and plan outfits for you.
  • Cons: The app itself is free, but the valuable features (styling) are paid services. Less focused on self-planning.
  • Best For: People with a budget who want expert help organizing their closet.

4. Pureple (The Budget Android Option)

The Verdict: Highly functional but clunky.

  • Pros: One of the few solid options for Android users historically. Auto-categorization features. Suggests outfits from your own closet.
  • Cons: Interface is very cluttered. The free version is ad-supported, which can be annoying.
  • Best For: Android users who want functionality over aesthetics.

5. FitWardrobe (The Smart, Private Choice)

The Verdict: A privacy-focused, cross-platform choice currently in Beta.

  • Pros: It‚Äôs a web app (PWA), meaning it works on any device (iOS, Android, Laptop). Privacy-first: your wardrobe data is designed to stay on your device. Features an AI Outfit Generator intended to assist with styling decisions.
  • Cons: As a newer entrant, it has fewer social features than Whering (which many users prefer for privacy).
  • Best For: Privacy-conscious users, minimalists, and anyone who wants AI styling without the bloat.
