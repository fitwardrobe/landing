Security at FitWardrobe

Organization: FitWardrobe

Architecture: Local-First, Privacy-Centric

Last Updated: February 3, 2026

Our Security Philosophy

At FitWardrobe, we believe the most secure data is the data we don't store. Our Local-First Architecture ensures that your wardrobe photos and inventory details stay on your device, not on our servers.

How We Protect You

1. Local Data Storage

Your wardrobe data is stored using browser IndexedDB or Capacitor Filesystem. It is never uploaded to the FitWardrobe cloud. This eliminates the risk of a central database breach exposing your personal photos.

2. Secure Authentication

We use Supabase (PostgreSQL + GoTrust) for authentication. We only store your email address and a secure password hash. We support login via Magic Links for passwordless, high-security access.

3. AI Processing Security

When you use our AI features, images are sent to the Google Gemini 2.0 Flash API over an encrypted HTTPS connection.

  • Images are processed in-memory.
  • Images are NOT used for training Google's models.
  • Images are NOT stored by us or Google after processing.

4. Encryption in Transit

All communication between your device and our services (Supabase, Gemini API, Vercel) is protected by TLS 1.2+ encryption (HTTPS).

Your Responsibilities

Since your data is stored locally, security also depends on you:

  • Device Security: Use a passcode, PIN, or biometric lock on your phone or computer.
  • Browser Security: Keep your browser updated and avoid untrusted extensions.
  • Backups: Regularly export your wardrobe data using the build-in "Export JSON" feature to prevent loss if your device is damaged.

Reporting Vulnerabilities

We welcome reports from security researchers. If you find a potential vulnerability in FitWardrobe, please email us at fitwardrobee@gmail.com. We will investigate and respond as quickly as possible.
